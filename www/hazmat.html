Hazmat

Employees have a right to know of any hazardous chemicals known to be present in their workplace that may be exposed under normal conditions of use or in a foreseeable emergency.Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) for every hazardous Material (HAZAMAT) must be readily available for employees to review during their work shift.  MSDS are compiled in yellow binders.  It is the supervisor’s responsibility to:

 

·         Inform each employee of the location of the MSDS binder(s).

·         Provide employee training on the proper use of the HAZMAT, storage and handling, disposal procedures, emergency procedures, labeling system of HAZMAT upon assignment.

·         Provide employees with Personal ProtectiveEquipment (PPE)

·         Provide employees with information on how to procure HAZMAT that is not currently approved for use in the work area.

 

For more information about NHB’s Hazard Communication Program contact your supervisor, Department EMS Coordinator, visit the NHB Environmental intranet web page:
